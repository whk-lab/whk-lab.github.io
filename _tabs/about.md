## 👋 About Me
Hi, I'm **Kevin-wh Kuo**, the creator of **WHK-Lab**. 

I am a Software Engineer deeply passionate about the intersection of **High-Performance Computing (HPC)** and **Large Language Models (LLM)**. My work focuses on pushing the boundaries of LLM inference efficiency and system-level optimizations.

## 🚀 What I Do
In this lab, I document my journey and technical deep dives into:
- **LLM Inference Engines**: Deep exploration of frameworks like `vLLM`, `sglang`, and `TensorRT-LLM`.
- **System Optimization**: Leveraging `torch.compile`, Triton kernels, and hardware-aware optimizations (NPU/GPU).
- **Performance Engineering**: Solving the "Memory Wall" and "Throughput Wall" in large-scale model serving.

## 💡 Why WHK-Lab?
I believe in **"Learning in Public"**. This blog serves as my external brain—a place where I synthesize complex technical papers and source code into actionable insights. By sharing these deep dives, I aim to contribute to the developer community and connect with like-minded engineers.
